## Part 1: Pricing Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: Premium Privacy Pricing

**Claim:** EchoPanel can command premium pricing (>$10/month) due to:
- Local-only architecture (unique differentiator)
- Mac-native experience (underserved market)
- Privacy-first positioning (resonates with technical professionals)

**Test:**
- Survey beta testers on willingness-to-pay
- A/B test pricing page at $8, $12, $15/month
- Monitor conversion rates at each price point

**Success Criteria:**
- 30% of users willing to pay $12+/month
- Conversion rate > 2% at $12/month
- No significant drop-off at $12 vs $10

### Hypothesis 2: Lifetime Pricing Appeal

**Claim:** Lifetime pricing ($99-149) will appeal to:
- Privacy-conscious users (pay once, own forever)
- Mac power users (comfortable with premium one-time purchases)
- Early adopters (reward early supporters)

**Test:**
- Survey interest in lifetime vs subscription
- A/B test lifetime option visibility
- Monitor lifetime purchase rate

**Success Criteria:**
- 40% prefer lifetime over monthly
- Lifetime conversion rate > 5% (when offered)
- Lifetime buyers have higher LTV than monthly

### Hypothesis 3: Annual Subscription Discount

**Claim:** Annual subscription with 20-30% discount will:
- Improve cash flow
- Increase user retention
- Be preferred by businesses

**Test:**
- Survey preference for monthly vs annual
- Offer 20% discount for annual
- Monitor annual purchase rate

**Success Criteria:**
- 50% prefer annual when shown 20% discount
- Annual conversion rate > 10%
- Annual churn rate < monthly churn rate

## Part 5: Pricing Experimentation

### Experiment 1: Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity

**Purpose:** Identify optimal price range and willingness-to-pay

**Method:** Ask 4 questions to beta testers:
1. At what price would EchoPanel be too expensive? (Too high)
2. At what price would EchoPanel be a bargain? (Too low)
3. At what price would EchoPanel be getting expensive? (Expensive but still buy)
4. At what price would EchoPanel be getting inexpensive? (Cheap but still buy)

**Analysis:**
- Plot price vs willingness-to-pay
- Identify optimal price point (intersection)
- Calculate price elasticity

### Experiment 2: Pricing Variant A/B Test

**Purpose:** Test different price points in market

**Method:** A/B test 3 pricing variants:
- Variant A: $8/month (penetration)
- Variant B: $12/month (market)
- Variant C: $15/month (premium)

**Duration:** 2 weeks minimum
**Traffic:** 50/50 split between variants

**Metrics:**
- Conversion rate by variant
- Average revenue per user (ARPU)
- Churn rate after 30 days
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)

### Experiment 3: Lifetime Option Visibility

**Purpose:** Test impact of lifetime option on conversion

**Method:** A/B test with and without lifetime option
- Variant A: Show lifetime option prominently
- Variant B: Hide lifetime option (only show subscription)

**Metrics:**
- Does lifetime option cannibalize subscriptions?
- Does lifetime option increase overall conversion?
- What is LTV difference between subscription and lifetime?

## Part 7: Pricing Strategy Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Three-Tier Pricing

**Structure:**
1. **Free Tier:** 14-day trial, limited to 20 meetings
2. **Professional:** $12/month ($96/year, 20% discount)
3. **Lifetime:** $99 one-time

**Rationale:**
- Free tier lowers friction
- Professional tier matches market premium
- Lifetime option differentiates from competitors
- Annual discount improves cash flow

### Recommendation 2: Lifetime Pricing at $99

**Strategy:**
- Promote lifetime as "early adopter special"
- Limit availability (first 500 buyers)
- Emphasize "pay once, own forever"

**Rationale:**
- $99 is < 8 months of Professional ($12 x 8 = $96)
- Creates urgency with limited availability
- Appeals to Mac power users (comfortable with one-time purchases)
- Differentiates from competitors who don't offer lifetime

### Recommendation 3: Annual Discount at 20%

**Strategy:**
- Show both monthly ($12) and annual ($96) options
- Highlight "Save 20%" prominently
- Default to annual on pricing page (can toggle)

**Rationale:**
- 20% discount is competitive (Otter offers ~17%)
- Improves cash flow
- Increases retention (annual customers stick longer)

### Recommendation 4: Premium Positioning

**Strategy:**
- Price at $12/month (above market average of $10)
- Justify premium with privacy + Mac-native differentiation
- Emphasize "no data monetization" vs competitors

**Rationale:**
- Privacy is worth premium to technical professionals
- Mac-native experience is underserved and premium
- Avoids race to bottom with cloud competitors

## Part 11: Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Price too high, low conversion

**Mitigation:**
- Start with penetration pricing ($8-10/month)
- A/B test multiple price points
- Be ready to adjust based on data
- Emphasize value (privacy + Mac-native)

### Risk: Lifetime option cannibalizes subscriptions

**Mitigation:**
- Monitor lifetime vs subscription mix
- If > 30% choose lifetime, raise lifetime price
- Test with and without lifetime option
- Limit lifetime availability (first 500 buyers)

### Risk: Competitors lower prices

**Mitigation:**
- Focus on differentiation (privacy, Mac-native)
- Don't compete on price alone
- Emphasize "no data monetization"
- Premium positioning vs commodity competitors

### Risk: Users don't value privacy

**Mitigation:**
- Survey privacy importance during beta
- If privacy not valued, pivot to other differentiation
- Test messaging variants (privacy vs convenience vs features)
